# Query pack: two-sided marketplaces & vertical SaaS for SMB operators

For marketplaces (connecting buyers/sellers or providers/customers) and
vertical SaaS sold to small-business operators (contractors, clinics,
salons, restaurants, agencies). Companies and Individuals are both common
here — the operator is often a reachable owner/operator, and the same
product may also be worth a partnership pitch to a platform or association.

## Query buckets

**Explicit demand**
- `"looking for software to" [job]` on industry-specific forums, Facebook
  groups, and subreddits for the trade/profession
- `"[competitor] alternative"` on G2, Capterra, and trade forums
- Industry association forums and Slack/Discord communities (public
  channels only) asking for tool recommendations

**Pain**
- `"double booking"`, `"manual invoicing"`, `"chasing payments"`,
  `"scheduling nightmare"` — operational pain specific to the trade
- G2/Capterra reviews of competitors mentioning the exact missing feature

**Workaround**
- Posts describing "we use [spreadsheet/paper/generic tool] for now" —
  strong Segment signal when it repeats across operators in the same trade
- Facebook group posts asking other operators how they handle a task the
  product automates

**Switching**
- `"[competitor] raised prices"`, `"[competitor] support is terrible"`,
  `"switching from [competitor]"` on G2, Capterra, Reddit, trade forums
- Public complaints in the competitor's own community/support forum

**Timing**
- Business expansion signals: new location opening, hiring posts for
  operations/admin roles, franchise announcements (Company-level timing)
- Seasonal timing specific to the trade (e.g. tax season for accounting
  tools, back-to-school for tutoring platforms)
- Platform/marketplace policy changes (a marketplace changing take-rate or
  policy creates both switching pain for sellers and a partnership timing
  signal for a competing platform)

## Source mix specific to this vertical

- G2, Capterra, TrustRadius reviews (both the product's own category and
  named competitors)
- Trade-specific forums, subreddits, and Facebook groups
- LinkedIn posts from operators and small-business owners
- Franchise and industry association public pages/newsletters
- Local business news covering expansions, openings, or closures (timing
  signal, especially for Company prospects)

## Classification notes

- A single-location owner-operator posting publicly is an **Individual**,
  even though they run a "company" — the test is always "can I reply to a
  specific person," and a solo operator almost always passes it.
- Reserve **Company** for the platform/marketplace/association itself (its
  seller base, its policy, its partnerships program) — not for one
  operator's business, however many employees they have.
